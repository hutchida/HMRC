title

Losses Available Corporation Tax Example

0

You must check the date from which these rules apply for the tax or duty you are dealing with. See CH81011 for full details.

1

Big Ltd, a company in a profitable group returns a trade loss of £10,000. It sets off £2,000 against its CT profits of the preceding period and surrenders £5,000 as Group Relief to Small Ltd, a fellow group company. The balance of £3,000 is carried forward for set off against expected future trade profits.

2

Big Ltd’s return is found to contain a careless inaccuracy. The true loss is £1,500. Big Ltd reduces its claim to set off the loss against its CT profits of the preceding period to £1,500 and withdraws the surrender of £5,000 as Group Relief.

3

It has not yet used the carried forward loss against its profits.

4

The potential lost revenue (PLR) for the careless inaccuracy, assuming liability at the small companies’ rate (say 21%) is

5

The original claim to Group Relief by Small Ltd was inaccurate as a result of the surrendering company’s incorrectly recorded loss. So we are not prevented from charging a penalty.
