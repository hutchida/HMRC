Poor behaviour by a minority of agents often falls into one or more of the following categories

1

unreasonable behaviour aimed at disrupting and delaying our checks

2

poor technical ability that leads to a loss of tax

3

suspicious repayment or registration activity that results in false repayments of tax

4

rude, abusive, threatening or violent behaviour

5

dishonest conduct.

6

There are ways we can respond to disruptive behaviour, usually by using our powers under FA08/Sch36.

7

We have established a simple reporting process so we can understand whether the reported issue is

8

a single event

9

part of a wider pattern of behaviour displayed by a particular agent, or

10

reflects a wider behavioural trend within the agent community, see CH860000.

We will not tolerate rudeness, abusive behaviour, violence or threats of violence against our staff. It is important that you and your manager follow the reporting process at HR62002 if you are subjected to abusive behaviour of this kind. You should also provide details of the agents behaviour to the (This content has been withheld because of exemptions in the Freedom of Information Act 2000) via the reporting process, see CH860000.

15
