COG932130

Action By SO Authorised Officer

0

The signing of directions and decisions is the responsibility of the SO authorised officer only. No other officer below the grade of SO is authorised to sign directions and decisions.

1

Before signing a direction and decision the SO authorised officer must review the case and be completely satisfied that there is sufficient evidence on which to form a reasoned opinion that the ‘employee has received relevant payments knowing that the employer has wilfully failed to deduct tax or pay the employee’s NIC’.

2

(This content has been withheld because of exemptions in the Freedom of Information Act 2000)

3

Where satisfied that a direction and decision is in order the SO authorised officer should sign and date the direction and decision in the following example format

4

‘Dated this twenty-third day of June 2010’.

5

The SO authorised officer should carefully record on the papers all the relevant factors taken into account and the reasons for deciding that a direction and decision was appropriate.

6

If the direction and decision are subsequently appealed see COG932810 onwards.

7

To make sure there is a digital record of the authorisation, the SO authorised officer must

8

use their caseflow login, and

9

upload a copy of the electronically signed direction and decision to Caseflow under the relevant caseflow reference.
