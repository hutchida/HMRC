CFM97390

Infrastructure: Interaction With The ‘De Minimis’ Provisions

0

TIOPA10/S443

1

In general, in a period of account where a worldwide group includes a qualifying infrastructure company (QIC), the de minimis provisions are not taken into account in calculating the interest capacity of that group.

2

However, there may be some circumstances in which this would limit a group’s deductions for tax-interest to less than the de minimis amount. There are therefore further rules to ensure that the group’s deductions for aggregate net tax-interest expense are never restricted below the de minimis amount.

3

These rules work by comparing:

4

the total disallowed amount of the group in the period calculated as if Chapter 8 applies (and therefore the de minimis is not available) – the ‘Chapter 8 amount’; and

5

the total disallowed amount of the group in the period calculated as if Chapter 8 was not applied, but interest capacity of the group was restricted to £2m i.e. the de minimis – the ‘ordinary amount’.

6

In an accounting period in which the Chapter 8 amount exceeds the ordinary amount, the rules in Chapter 8 are switched off and the interest capacity is taken to be the de-minimis amount.

7

However, from 1 January 2018, these rules do not apply to a worldwide group that has QIC (Q) that receives tax-interest income (that is not insignificant) from another QIC which is not in the same worldwide group as Q but which is a related party of Q. Where this applies the group must apply the infrastructure rules to Q.

8

Example

9

Company A and Company B are in a group together. Company B is a QIC.

10

Column ‘No Chapter 8’ reflects interest restriction which would arise if Company B had not made an election to be a QIC which had effect for the accounting period. Interest capacity of the group would be £3m, by reference to the fixed ratio method.

11

However, Company B is a QIC. The Chapter 8 amount for the group would therefore be £1.5m, as there is no tax-EBITDA in Company A to generate interest capacity and the de minimis is not available.

12

The ordinary amount would be £0.5m. As this is lower than the Chapter 8 amount, the interest capacity of the group will be £2m for this accounting period.

13

Application of the above exception is not considered a revocation, and as such a comparison of the Chapter 8 amount against the ordinary amount may be required accounting period on period.
