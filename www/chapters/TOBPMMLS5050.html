Granting A Licence

0

Each successful applicant will be issued with a licence which will allow them to carry out regulated activities in the UK. The licence must legally contain the following information:

1

a unique licence number

2

the name and address of the individual/company approved under the scheme.

3

the company name (if applicable) and company registration number (if applicable)

4

a description of the regulated activity/activities permitted. (ie manufacture, purchase, acquire, own or be in possession of an item of tobacco products manufacturing machinery importing or exporting).

5

the date the licence giving approval was granted, and its expiry date. The approval can be granted for up to a maximum of two years. If there are reasons for believing that a shorter time-frame should be granted initially, this needs to be confirmed during the application and assurance process. If restrictions to a licence are required, the licence will be granted for an initial twelve month period.

6

confirmation of each tobacco products manufacturing machine held including its specific identification number

7

the purpose and tobacco products manufactured by the machine(s) held. This will have been confirmed as part of the application process.

8

the address(es) at which the approved individual/company will keep the tobacco products manufacturing machines . This may be more than one address and will be specific to the agreed locations for each specific machine.

9

confirmation of any parts held and the frequency those parts are received

10

any prescribed conditions of the approval.

11

12

The applicant should be advised that the licence must be kept and be available for inspection upon request from HMRC or Border Force.
