title

Loans To Participators: Release Or Writing Off Of Loan Or Advance: Class 1 NIC

0

As well as attracting a potential charge under ITTOIA05/S415, where the participator or associate is an employee, the amount released or written off will attract Class 1 NIC if it is remuneration or profit derived from an employment (Section 3 (1) SSCBA 1992). This was confirmed in the First Tier Tribunal in Stewart Fraser Ltd v Commissioners for HMRC [2011] UKFTT 46 (TC).

1

Therefore when you find that a loan to which Section 455 applied has been wholly or partly written off or released, you should make a report, by e-mail, to RIS, Glasgow Intell Referrals (RIS). This report should:

2

refer to ITTOIA05/S415, to SSCBA92/S3 (1) and to this paragraph,

3

give the full name and address (and the tax reference, if known) of the person to whom the loan etc was made,

4

give the date the loan etc was released or written off and the amount released or written off,

5

give the name of the company that made the loan or advance

6

For the treatment on the death of the individual to whom the loan was made see CTM61665.

7

For the treatment on the termination of a trust see CTM61670.

8

For sums paid into settlement see ITTOIA05/S633; this treatment is not followed if Section 633 applies to the loan.
