title

Penalties: Special Reduction

0

Where you decide to assess a penalty for dishonesty that is the minimum amount of £5,000 you must consider special reduction before you have the penalty authorised.

1

You will not consider special reduction if the penalty is more than £5,000.

2

Technical guidance about special reduction starts at CH170000.

3

The individual may ask you to consider a special reduction. If they do you should

4

explain that we can only give a special reduction in special circumstances

5

get full details of why they consider that special reduction is due.

6

You must seek all information that you consider relevant to whether there are special circumstances. You can use information powers under FA08/Sch36 to do this.

7

When deciding whether a special reduction should be allowed you must consider

8

whether special circumstances are present, see CH170600

9

all circumstances which you have become aware of during your investigation

10

whether you need to further clarify anything to establish a special circumstance.

11

In most cases you will conclude that there are no special circumstances. You must make a note of the reasons for that decision on the case records and write to the individual to explain why you consider that a special reduction is not due.

12

You must not enter into discussions regarding the amount of any special reduction.

13
