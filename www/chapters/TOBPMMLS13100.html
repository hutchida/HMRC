Reviewing/Appealing Against The Imposition Of A Penalty

0

In normal circumstances the penalty is assessed on a person or a decision issued to the person and any appeal and review rights are restricted to that person.

A person has appeal and review rights against our decision to impose a penalty.

9

If a person does not want a review, they may appeal to the tribunal. If they ask for a review but are not satisfied with the conclusion they have a further 30 days within which to appeal to the tribunal.

10

However, they may not appeal to the tribunal until a review taking place under Finance Act 1994 Section 15C has been concluded.

11

Any appeal to the Tribunal must be made within 30 days beginning with the conclusion date

12

13
