Example: Claim By Company Owned By A Consortium From A Company In The Same Group As A Member Of The Consortium

0

The consortium and group structure is as shown in the diagram at CTM80590.

1

The percentages shown in the diagram indicate beneficial ownership of ordinary share capital and you should assume that the arrangements rules (CTA10/Ss146A, 146B, and 155) do not apply.

2

The votes and entitlement to profits and assets on winding up follow the shareholdings shown (see CTA10/Ss151(4) for the 75%+ and 90%+ subsidiaries (CTM80535), and Ss143(3) and 144(3) for the ownership proportions of L and X in CH (CTM80540 and CTM80545)).

3

Apportionments are on a time basis (see CTM80260 for alternative methods).

4

Facts

5

CH is a holding company owned by members of a consortium L (60%) and X (40%). CH holds 90% of the ordinary share capital of CT1 a trading subsidiary. CT2 and CT3 are 100% trading subsidiaries of CH.

6

L owns 75% of the shares in S2. The other 25% are not held by any company in P’s group.

7

This part of the consortium and group structure has existed for many years.

8

All the companies except S2 draw up accounts to 31 December.

9

For accounting periods to 31 December 2010:

10

CT1 has profits of £40,000.

11

CT2 and CT3 each have losses of £3,000.

12

CH has neither profit nor loss.

13

S2 has losses of £19,000 for accounting period 12 months to 30 June 2011.

14

CT1 makes a consortium claim in respect of S2’s losses.

15

CTA10/S149(2) and (3) (CTM80585) applies because there is the potential for CT1 to make group claims in respect of CT2’s and CT3’s losses. It could claim £6,000 in total, £3,000 from each. So for the purposes of calculating the amount of consortium relief CT1 can claim, its total profits are £34,000 (£40,000 minus £6,000).

16

Procedure

17

The provisions about apportionment to overlapping periods (CTA10/Ss138 to 142 - CTM80215) interact with those about consortium shares (CTA10/S144 - CTM80545) and those about surrenders by group members CTA10/Ss133 and 145 - CTM80560. A restriction is also needed because the consortium company could claim make group claims from fellow group companies (CTA10/S149 - CTM80585).

18

The claim

19

CT1 can claim consortium relief from S2 because S2 is in the same group as the link company L. CTA10/S133 applies (CTM80550).

20

The overlapping period (CTM80225) is the six months ended 31 December 2010.

21

S2’s surrenderable amount for the accounting period ended 30 June 2011 is £19,000.

22

S2 has made no prior surrenders so its ‘unused part of the surrenderable amounts’ is the same as its ‘surrenderable amount for the overlapping period’ (CTM80230). It is:

23

CT1’s total profits for its accounting period to 31 December 2010 are £34,000 as above. CTA10/S144 (CTM80545), however, limits this to L’s share (‘ownership proportion’ CTA10/S145(3) - CTM80560) of CT1’s profits for the accounting period.

24

This is:

25

So CT1’s ‘unrelieved part of the claimant company’s available total profits’ is the same as its ‘available total profits for the overlapping period’ as it has made no prior claims; it is:

26

The amount that can be surrendered/claimed is the smaller of £9,500 and £9,180: £9,180.
