FRS6100

Anti-Avoidance Rules: What Are The Anti-Avoidance Exclusions?

0

The FRS is intended for small stand-alone businesses and the anti-avoidance exclusions mean:

1

Businesses that are eligible for group treatment, or are registered for VAT as a divisional registration, at the time of application - or have been in the preceding 24 months - are excluded.

2

This is designed to reduce the threat of exploitation by larger companies.

3

Businesses that acquire or intend to acquire capital items that are covered by the capital goods scheme are excluded.

4

Analysis showed that the treatment of FRS businesses as fully taxable presented the potential for abuse by exempt companies and this exclusion is to prevent such avoidance schemes from developing.

5

Businesses that are associated - or have been in the preceding 24 months - are excluded.

6

This is a catch-all provision to prevent avoidance and abuse. Further details can be found at FRS6200.
