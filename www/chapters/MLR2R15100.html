Regulation 27(4)(a) requires MLR businesses to notify us of any changes to their registration details within 30 days of the change occurring, or within 30 days of the date the business became aware of the change.

1

Notification will be in the form of a letter from the MLR business.

2

Compliance visits and Interventions can also establish a failure to notify changes. In such cases, you will receive a report from the Officer informing you of this fact and giving any details that they established. You must ensure you have confirmation of the details from the business before updating DTR and issuing a new certificate.

3

In many cases failure to notify changes to registration details will be the result of error or neglect.

4

See Penalties guidance MLR1 for guidance on when a warning letter or penalty should be issued for failure to notify us of registration detail changes.

Regulation 57(4) requires MLR businesses to notify us of any material change or inaccurracy to their registration details within 30 days of the change occurring or discovery of the inaccuracy. HMRC may agree an extension to this time in certain circumstances.

1

These changes, among others include:

2

key persons in the business such as directors, partners or owners

3

criminal convictions for relevant offences in the business listed in schedule 3 of the regulations

4

additional premises or agents

5

premises that are no longer used for business activities covered by the Regulations.

6

See Guidance for more information
